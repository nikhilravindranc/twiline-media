01 — Identity

The
mark.

A geometric TM monogram constructed from pure right-angle forms. No curves. No decoration. Every pixel earns its place — like the brand it represents.

Color Variants
On Dark
On Light
On Gold
Usage Rules
Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the T nub on all four sides. No other elements inside this zone.

Minimum Size

Digital: 32px height minimum. Print: 10mm height minimum. Below these sizes, the mark loses legibility.

Never Do

Do not rotate, stretch, recolour, add drop shadows, place on busy backgrounds, or recreate with different geometry.

Approved Colours

White (#EEECE7) on dark. Dark (#252321) on light. Dark (#252321) on gold only. No other colour combinations.

Icon Construction Rules
Grid

All icons drawn on a 24×24px grid with 2px padding. Stroke weight: 1.5px. Never scale stroke with transform.

Geometry

Square line caps. Miter joins. No rounded corners. No decorative curves. The same discipline as the TM mark.

Colour

Default: #EEECE7 stroke on dark. #252321 stroke on light. Gold (#FFD700) used for one accent element maximum per icon.

Scale

24px standard UI. 32px card use. 48px hero use. Never render below 20px — detail is lost.

05 — Photography

Cinematic.
Atmospheric.

Our photography language is drawn from narrative filmmaking, not commercial photography. Every frame has a point of view. Every shot has emotional weight.

Five Principles
Atmospheric depth
01 — Presence
Backs, not faces.
Subjects rarely face the camera. Silhouettes, profiles, partial reveals. Human presence within landscape — not dominating it.
Chiaroscuro light
02 — Light
One source. Total control.
Dramatic contrast between light and shadow. A single strong source — window, sun, lamp. Rim lighting, golden shafts, deep blacks.
Cinematic depth
03 — Atmosphere
Fog. Mist. Depth.
Multiple depth planes — blurred foreground, mid-ground subject, layered background. Atmosphere is a character in the frame.
Tactile detail
04 — Detail
Texture as narrative.
Extreme close-ups carry emotional weight — hands on grass, an eye through planks, a flower's macro geometry. Physical texture tells the story.
Tonal temperature
05 — Colour
Cool or warm. Never neither.
Either deeply cool (teal/blue/grey) or deeply warm (amber/gold). Not desaturated neutrals. The colour temperature is a deliberate editorial choice.
Tonal Palette
Two modes. One brand.

Do / Don't
Shoot like this
  • Subject facing away or in profile, integrated into environment
  • Fog, mist, or haze creating atmospheric depth
  • Dramatic single light source — window, sunrise, streetlamp
  • Cinematic aspect ratios — 16:9, 2.39:1 letterbox
  • Macro tactile close-ups — hands, eyes, texture, grain
  • Human figure small within a large landscape
  • Cool teal or warm amber — committed to one temperature
Never this
  • Direct-to-camera smiling product shots
  • Flat even lighting — studio white background photography
  • Oversaturated or HDR-processed images
  • Stock photography clichés — handshakes, laptops in cafes
  • Split toning that reads as Instagram filter
  • Text overlaid on busy imagery without contrast control
  • Mixed colour temperatures in the same campaign

07 — Content Rules

Global rules.
Every format.

Format-specific rules that govern how Twiline communicates across every channel. These rules are not guidelines — they are the standard.

Social Posts
  • Hook in line 1 — earn the expand before anything else
  • One idea per post — never dilute with secondary messages
  • End with a question or a provocative statement
  • Max 3 targeted hashtags — no hashtag walls
  • Sentence max: 18 words
Ad Copy
  • Problem in headline — not the solution
  • Proof in body — specific number or concrete descriptor
  • CTA in gold — every ad answers "what changes if I click?" in 3 seconds
  • Headline: 6–10 words max
  • Never start body copy with "We"
Proposals
  • Lead with the client's problem — not Twiline's capabilities
  • Capabilities are proof, not pitch
  • Close with next steps, not features
  • One clear ask per proposal
  • No more than 2 pages for initial outreach
Email
  • Subject line: max 7 words, no clickbait
  • One primary CTA per email — never two
  • Cold outreach: 3 short paragraphs maximum
  • Never open with "I hope this email finds you well"
  • Plain text preferred over heavy HTML for cold email
Video Scripts
  • Hook: first 3 seconds — no exceptions
  • Conflict: seconds 4–15
  • Resolution + CTA: final 5 seconds
  • Reading pace: 130 words per minute
  • Write for ears, not eyes — short sentences, no jargon
Investor Decks
  • One idea per slide — no paragraph-heavy slides
  • Narrative arc: problem → insight → solution → proof → ask
  • Data in charts, not tables
  • The ask slide must name a specific number
  • Max 12 slides for seed/Series A decks
Globally Banned Words

These words are prohibited across all Twiline content in all markets. No exceptions.

11 — Future

Ready before
expansion.

Future markets are documented now — not when expansion happens. The brand voice, tone, and cultural register for each market is established and waiting. We arrive prepared, not reactive.

The brands that define their voice across borders before they cross them — own the market when they arrive.
Twiline's current focus is India — pan-India, with Kerala as the home-adjacent priority. Every future market listed below has its tone calibrated, its cultural register understood, and its content rules defined. When the time comes, we don't build from scratch. We execute.
Future Markets
🇦🇪
UAE & Middle East
High formality · Medium warmth · Low urgency · AED · Vision 2031

Formal, aspirational, relationship-first. UAE business culture values prestige and long-term positioning. Build authority slowly — earn trust first. Arabic only with native verification.

"In a region building its next decade, your brand's position today determines where you stand when the market matures."
🇨🇦
Canada
Medium formality · Very high warmth · Low urgency · CAD · PIPEDA

Warm-direct. American directness meets British politeness. Inclusive framing resonates strongly — diversity and community impact. Warmth matters more than urgency.

"Growth doesn't have to feel aggressive. The best campaigns start with a simple question: what do your customers actually need to hear?"

United Kingdom
Med-high formality · Dry wit · Very low urgency · £ · ICO/GDPR

Understatement and dry wit. Overpromising reads as gauche. Prove through case study, not claim. Self-deprecating precision is more trusted than confident assertion.

"We don't promise transformation. We deliver campaigns that work — which, frankly, is rarer than it should be."
